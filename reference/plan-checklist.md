# plan-checklist.md — the anatomy of a good AI-upskilling rollout

The citable, objective standard Andra points to when it flags a gap — the AP-Stylebook
analog: a plan can be checked against this rather than against Andra's unsupported
opinion. Every row traces to a specific mechanism in `andragogy.md`,
`change-management.md`, `resistance.md`, or `heuristics.md`. This file is a checklist,
not new content — it does not introduce any claim not already sourced elsewhere in
`reference/`.

---

## Content layer (single session) — `andragogy.md`

| Check | Trace |
|---|---|
| Opens with the learner's task/payoff, not the tool's features | Principle 1 — Need to Know; `heuristics.md § Curse of Mastery` |
| Language and exercises treat the learner as a domain expert adding a capability | Principle 2 — Self-Concept |
| At least one exercise requires the learner's own judgment to complete, not just execution | Principle 3 — Prior Experience |
| Names a specific, current, recurring pain point (not swappable across audiences) | Principle 4 — Readiness |
| Outline/structure organized by task, not by tool feature | Principle 5 — Problem-Centered |
| At least one early hands-on moment using the learner's own material | Principle 6 — Intrinsic Motivation; `heuristics.md § Engineer Early Wins` |
| No unbroken content stretch longer than ~20 minutes without re-anchoring | `heuristics.md § 20-Minute Attention Ceiling` |

## Arc layer (the rollout, across sessions) — `change-management.md`

| Check | Trace |
|---|---|
| States why this change, why now, before diving into content | ADKAR — Awareness |
| Has a distinct, scheduled application/practice session, not just a mention of "go try it" | `heuristics.md § Sit-and-Get vs. Apply` |
| Gap between concept session and application follow-up is roughly a week | ADKAR — Reinforcement; `heuristics.md § Forgetting-Curve Follow-up` |
| Follow-up session has a different job than the first (troubleshoot/apply, not re-teach) | Change-management § The follow-up gap |
| Protects actual time for practice rather than assuming the learner finds it independently | `heuristics.md § Time-Scarcity Tax` |
| Names a plan for institutionalizing the new behavior past the rollout (process update, removed fallback, recognition) | Lewin — Refreeze |
| Identifies champions/managers to sustain reinforcement, not just the single presenter | Change-management § Manager/champion involvement |

## Framing layer (psychological safety and adoption) — `resistance.md`

| Check | Trace |
|---|---|
| Opens simple/guided, not with the tool's full complexity | TAM — Perceived Ease of Use |
| Explicitly names which tasks the tool absorbs vs. which require continued human judgment | Augmentation-not-replacement |
| No language that frames the tool as replacing the role rather than absorbing tasks | Augmentation-not-replacement |
| Addresses at least one of the four real resistance sources explicitly, rather than assuming goodwill | Resistance § the four real sources |
| No age/generation-targeted language; hesitancy framed behaviorally, not demographically | Age and generational dynamics |

## How Andra uses this file

RULE-05/06/07 diagnostic passes work through the relevant section of this checklist
against the actual plan text. RULE-08 then prioritizes: a plan typically fails several
rows at once, and the highest-leverage flags are usually the ones with the widest blast
radius (a missing follow-up structure undermines even a well-designed single session) —
see `change-management.md § The follow-up gap` for the reasoning on why that row in
particular tends to outrank content-layer flags.
